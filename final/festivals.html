Maskara Festival: From City of Smiles

The MassKara Festival is an annual celebration held in Bacolod City, Philippines, every October. It's known for its vibrant street dances, colorful masks, and upbeat music, and is a symbol of Bacolod's resilience and joyful spirit. The festival draws both local and international tourists, making it a major event in the Philippine

Key Aspects of the MassKara Festival:

Origins:

The festival began in the 1980s during a period of hardship, with the goal of lifting the spirits of the people and showcasing Bacolod's resilience.

Name:

"MassKara" is a combination of "mass" (meaning a large crowd) and "cara" (meaning face), referring to the smiling masks worn by performers.

Activities:

The festival features a street dance competition, where dancers in elaborate costumes and masks perform in the city's streets and plazas. Other activities include food fairs, concerts, beauty pageants, and sports competitions.

Locations:

Major events are typically held in the Bacolod Public Plaza and the Lacson Tourism Strip.

Significance:

The MassKara Festival is a symbol of Bacolod's ability to overcome challenges and celebrate its unique culture and joyful spirit.
