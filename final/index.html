Adobo is a popular Filipino dish made by marinating meat—usually chicken or pork—in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns.

The MassKara Festival is a colorful and joyful festival held every October in Bacolod City, Philippines. The name comes from the words "mass" (many people) and "kara" (face).

© Louis Naingue

Palawan is a beautiful island province in the southwest part of the Philippines, known for its white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, limestone cliffs.

© Louis Naingue

The Kapre is a creature from Philippine folklore. It is usually described as a giant, hairy, dark-skinned being that lives in large trees, especially balete or mango trees.
