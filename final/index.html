EXPLORE THE PHILIPPINES

The Philippines is a beautiful country in Southeast Asia made up of more than 7,000 islands. It is known for its stunning beaches, friendly people, rich culture, and delicious food. The country has three major island groups:

  • Luzon

    • This is the largest island and home to the capital city, Manila. It is the center of government, business, and education. Luzon also has famous natural spots like the Banaue Rice Terraces and Mount Mayon.

  • Visayas

    • Located in the middle of the country, this island group is known for its beautiful white sand beaches, clear waters, and vibrant festivals. Popular places include Cebu, Bohol, and Boracay.

  • Mindanao

    • The second-largest island, found in the south, is rich in natural resources and agriculture. It has a mix of cultures and is known for places like Davao City and Mount Apo, the highest mountain in the Philippines.

PHILIPPINE ICONIC JEEP

The jeepney is one of the most famous symbols of the Philippines. Originally made from U.S. military jeeps left after World War II, jeepneys were transformed into colorful and creative public transport vehicles. They are known for their bright designs, long seats, and open-air style.

Jeepneys are called the “King of the Road” because they are a popular and affordable way for Filipinos to get around, especially in cities and towns. Each jeepney has a specific route, and passengers hop on and off easily. The inside is often full of music, conversation, and local life.

FEATURED TODAY

Famous Adobo

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish made by marinating meat—usually chicken or pork—in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns.

Maskara Festival

The MassKara Festival is a colorful and joyful festival held every October in Bacolod City, Philippines. The name comes from the words "mass" (many people) and "kara" (face).

Palawan

Palawan is a beautiful island province in the southwest part of the Philippines, known for its white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, limestone cliffs.

Kapre-Creatrue

The Kapre is a creature from Philippine folklore. It is usually described as a giant, hairy, dark-skinned being that lives in large trees, especially balete or mango trees.
